# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

B2B professionals in the New York construction ecosystem: developers, general contractors sourcing electrical specialists, project managers evaluating subcontractors, and institutional investors performing due diligence on construction partners. They arrive informed, scan fast, and decide based on evidence of capability, not brand storytelling. Primary action: assess, then contact immediately.

## Product Purpose

Meridian Build's website converts a professional visitor's initial skepticism into a request for contact, in under 90 seconds. It does this through evidence, not claims: an interactive project explorer that lets visitors navigate completed work spatially, a precise and sparse visual identity, and zero padding copy. The site must feel like the company built it — structured, precise, no wasted space.

## Brand Personality

Precise. Institutional. Confident.

## Anti-references

- Generic contractor sites with safety-vest yellow/orange, stock hard-hat imagery, and icon card grids
- Consumer-facing home improvement platforms (HomeAdvisor, Angi) — this is B2B only
- Overcrowded portfolio sites that substitute quantity for quality
- Dark "aggressive" construction designs that trade authority for visual drama
- Template-based sites that look like any other service business

## Design Principles

1. Evidence over assertion. Never claim what can be shown.
2. Precision in everything. A misaligned element communicates the same thing as a crooked wall.
3. Respect the visitor's time. Every word on screen is competing for attention from a busy professional.
4. The interaction IS the portfolio. The building explorer doesn't describe work — it demonstrates the level of craft the company operates at.
5. NYC register. Dense, efficient, expert. No hand-holding.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA minimum. The building explorer must have full keyboard navigation (Tab, Enter, Space) and a textual fallback for non-visual contexts. All animations must respect prefers-reduced-motion. Form fields must have explicit labels, not placeholder-only labels.
